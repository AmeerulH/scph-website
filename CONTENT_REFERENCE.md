### What is Planetary Health?

> A planetary health approach to human development recognises that humankind has made significant progress in many ways with the industrial, green and technological revolutions as examples. But these development gains are now being offset by increasingly obvious disruption to the health of the planet. We can see this in depleted biodiversity, changing land use and cover, a massive increase in air pollution, shortages of natural resources, and the resulting damage to our lived environment most clearly demonstrated through recent pandemic and disease outbreaks and the climate emergency.

### Planetary Health Roadmap and Action Plan

> The Planetary Health Roadmap and Action Plan was unveiled as the primary outcome of the 2024 Planetary Health Summit and 6th Annual Meeting (PHAM 2024). In today's world, marked by escalating environmental challenges and their impact on human well-being, the Roadmap and Action Plan can help to answer the question: "How do we 'do' Planetary Health?"
>
> The development of this Roadmap and Action Plan was made possible through the collaborative efforts of experts working across four working groups and includes more than 100 experts from prestigious institutions around the planet.
>
> The document aims to bridge Planetary Health discourse between academia and action via policy, political, and civil society spaces, and to begin the process of pulling together an impactful set of actions that address the interconnected issues of human and environmental health. The Roadmap addresses the pressing need for a coordinated global response to the environmental challenges that threaten human health and biodiversity.

## NEWS / ARTICLES PAGE
Source: https://sunwayuniversity.edu.my/research/planetaryhealth/news

### Articles Published

1. **'We are at War with Nature' – and We Urgently Need to Make Peace**
   - On biodiversity loss (e.g. Malayan tiger extinction) as a step toward human extinction.

2. **Pathways Linking Climate Change and HIV/AIDS: An Updated Conceptual Framework and Implications for the Philippines**
   - Reviews the HIV-climate nexus framework; connection between climate change and HIV/AIDS is still underrecognised in research and practice.

3. **Beyond the Hippocratic Oath: A Planetary Health Pledge for the Malaysian Medical Community**
   - We live in the Anthropocene Age; without care for the planet, human health and development are compromised.

4. **Safeguarding Human Health in the Anthropocene Epoch**
   - Far-reaching changes to Earth's natural systems represent a growing threat to human health, even as global health has mainly improved.

5. **Tackling the Politics of Intersectoral Action for the Health of People and Planet**
   - Unlocking the potential for intersectoral action on climate and health requires thinking politically about facilitators and barriers.

6. **Plastics Are A Planetary Health Crisis – Can You Do Anything About It?**
   - References the UN Environment Assembly resolution in Nairobi (Feb–March 2022) calling for a plastics treaty.

7. **The Future Belongs to the Youth, It's Time We Listen to Them**
   - Malaysia is poorly prepared for the larger planetary health crisis.

8. **Indonesia: Why Planetary Health Should Be on the Menu for the G20 Chair**
   - At the World Economic Forum in Geneva (January), Indonesia's President focused on strengthening global health architecture.

9. **Health of Our Planet Impacts Our Own Wellness**
   - Joint column with Tan Sri Dr Jemilah Mahmood (SCPH executive director) and Health Minister Khairy Jamaluddin on the urgency of planetary health.

10. **Malaysia is Drowning in Uneaten Food – 6 Steps You Can Take to Help Stop Food Waste**
    - Food waste is a serious element of the planetary health crisis.

11. **Tackling Air Pollution in the Philippines**
    - Three decades after the country's Clean Air Act, the Philippines is still far from achieving comprehensive air pollution control.

12. **Financing the Future of WHO**
    - WHO's resources have consistently lagged behind its constitutional mandate; deep misalignment between expectations and resources.
